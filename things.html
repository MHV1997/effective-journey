Hiking

Hiking routes abound in the neighbouring Horton Plains National Park, allowing tourists to discover the varied flora and animals as they make their way towards the waterfall. There are many opportunities to see animals while hiking through verdant forests and tea gardens on the approach to Bambarakanda.

Hiking routes near the Diyaluma waterfall go over undulating hills, tea plantations, and lush scenery. The walk frequently takes you across steep terrain and up to numerous waterfall levels, offering you diverse viewpoints and swimming holes.

The hiking routes that surround Ravana Falls take hikers over steep terrain and deep forests in the direction of the falls. Hiking may include a descent to the waterfall's base pool, which offers an up-close view of the tumbling water and surrounding rocky formations.

Sightseeing

In addition to the waterfall itself, there are sweeping vistas of the neighbouring valleys, tea plantations, and far-off mountains that may be seen from above. The drive to Bambarakanda is a picturesque and engrossing experience that highlights the hill highlands of Sri Lanka.

Tourists may take in expansive views of the gushing river, verdant surroundings, and far-off landscapes from various vantage points along the paths. The Diyaluma Waterfall region is well known for its unspoiled beauty, providing lots of chances for leisure and photography in peaceful settings.

Viewers are treated to a gorgeous backdrop of misted mountains, verdant valleys, and tea plantations encircling Ravana Falls. The region's cultural importance, which is connected to the Ramayana mythology, enhances the tourist experience and provides information about the rich cultural legacy of Sri Lanka.
