Bambarakanda Waterfalls

The highest waterfall in the island country is Bambarakanda waterfall, which is located in the Badulla area of Sri Lanka. It drops an astounding 263 metres (863 ft) to the ground. This natural treasure, which is tucked away in the steep terrain of Horton Plains National Park, enthrals tourists with its stunning beauty and immaculate surroundings.
The Kuda Oya, a branch of the Walawe River, supplies the waterfall's water, which cascades down a steep rock face into a deep, rocky pool below. Bambarakanda's gorgeous environment is enhanced by the rich vegetation and varied flora surrounding it, which attracts many hikers and nature lovers.
The trip to Bambarakanda is a beautiful experience that offers sweeping vistas of rolling hills and tea farms as you travel through the hill country's twisting roadways. Trekking through lush scenery is typically required to reach the waterfall, offering a deeply immersive experience in the midst of Sri Lanka's breathtaking natural splendour. Bambarakanda is a must-visit location for individuals seeking peace among breathtaking scenery because of the quiet ambiance and the cool mist that comes from the falling water.

Diyaluma Waterfalls

One of Sri Lanka's most well-known natural landmarks is the Diyaluma waterfall, which is situated in the Badulla area close to the town of Koslanda. Perched in the scenic Hill Country, with a spectacular cascade of around 220 metres (720 feet), it is the second-highest waterfall in Sri Lanka.
Diyaluma is a waterfall that creates numerous levels of shimmering water that flows over a variety of rock formations. It is fed by the Punagala Oya, a tributary of the Walawe River. The falls are surrounded by rich vegetation, which heightens the enchanted atmosphere and makes it a well-liked tourist and local attraction.
Hiking through the rolling hills leads to Diyaluma, which offers expansive views of the valleys and tea gardens around. While many people are drawn to the waterfall's natural pools for soothing dips, others are more interested in the exhilarating experience of cliff jumping from the rocky ledges.
The Diyaluma Waterfall region is abundant in biodiversity, with a wide variety of plants and animals flourishing in the pure surroundings. As a result, the waterfall acts as a haven for nature enthusiasts looking for peace amidst the splendour of Sri Lanka's Hill Country in addition to being a picturesque site.

Ravana Falls

Named after the mythical character from the Hindu epic Ramayana, Ravana Falls is a stunning waterfall in Sri Lanka's Badulla District, namely in the Ella region. Falling from a height of around 25 metres (82 feet), the waterfall produces an amazing show as it tumbles into a pool that is encircled by rocky outcrops and thick vegetation.
In accordance with regional legend, Princess Sita was abducted by the legendary King Ravana of Lanka and imprisoned there, according to the legend surrounding Ravana Falls. The waterfall's connection to this story enhances its attraction, drawing tourists drawn by the area's rich cultural past.
It is not too difficult to reach Ravana Falls; a little climb from the main road leads to a viewing point with expansive views of the gushing water. Adventuresome tourists frequently go closer to the waterfall, swimming in the cool pool or exploring the surrounding area.
Along with its natural beauty, the region surrounding Ravana Falls is also known for its lush valleys, tea plantations, and mist-covered mountains. Whether viewed from afar or experienced up close, Ravana Falls enthrals tourists with the beauty of nature and the mystery surrounding its fabled beginnings.
